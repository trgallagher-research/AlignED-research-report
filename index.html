AlignED: Benchmarking AI Models on Professional Teaching Tasks

AlignED Benchmark Project

February 2026 · Living document

Abstract

Teachers are adopting generative AI faster than any previous educational technology. OECD TALIS 2024 data shows 41% of lower secondary teachers across participating countries have used AI for professional tasks. Yet there is a shortage of evaluations that test models on the educational tasks they are currently being used for: identifying misconceptions about learning, diagnosing why teaching strategies fail, answering the kinds of questions that appear on teacher certification exams, and comparing student work against curriculum standards.

AlignED addresses this gap with five evaluations spanning neuromyth identification, diagnostic reasoning, teacher certification knowledge (general pedagogy and inclusive education), and student work judgement. Up to 32 models from five providers have been tested. Key findings: no single model ranks first across all evaluations; performance on one benchmark does not predict performance on another; assessment task type matters enormously, with models performing well on classification tasks but poorly on absolute grading; and all models exhibit universal overconfidence, never expressing uncertainty even when wrong.

This site is a living document. Benchmarks, model pools, and results are updated as new evaluations are completed.

Reading guide

This site is structured like an academic paper. The Introduction frames the problem and reviews related work. Methods describes all five evaluations and their scoring. Results presents findings with charts and stories from the data. The Discussion draws out five takeaways, states limitations, and outlines future work. Appendices contain full item lists, data access, and citation information.
